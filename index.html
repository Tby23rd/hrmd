About The Company

The placement of a vehicle’s head restraint is critical to mitigating common soft-tissue neck injuries that often result from a collision. In 1995, the Insurance Corporation of British Columbia (ICBC) in collaboration with Rona Kinetics of North Vancouver, commissioned the first Head Restraint Measuring Device or HRMD to provide an accurate and repeatable method to measure head restraint position. Today they are an integral tool to comply with motor vehicle regulations around the world.

In 2015 HRMD was incorporated to streamline customer service with a one stop shop for the following: 1) Supply of new HRMD’s 2) Re-calibration of existing systems 3) Repair of HRMD’s 4) Calibration of HRMD’s together with the SAE J-826 Manikin per VDA 304 and EuroNCAP-Gloria
